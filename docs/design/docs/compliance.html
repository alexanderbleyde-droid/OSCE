Plexus OSCE Stations — Compliance Requirements#

Scope: the requirements every station must satisfy to be valid for use in the Plexus OSCE engine. "Compliance" here means three things together: clinical safety, assessment validity, and content governance. Requirements are derived from MASTER_STATION_BLUEPRINT.md and the two reference stations.

A station may not be published until every MUST in this document is satisfied and signed off.

Not legal or regulatory advice. This document covers the clinical-governance and assessment-validity requirements internal to the product. Formal regulatory questions — medical-device classification in any jurisdiction, data-protection/GDPR obligations, and exam-board or accreditation requirements — must be confirmed with the client's own legal and regulatory advisors. Treat the items below as the engineering/clinical contract, not a regulatory clearance.

0. Product framing (applies to every station)#

  • MUST present the platform as a training and assessment simulation. The simulated patient is synthetic. The platform is not a medical device, not clinical decision support, and not a source of medical advice for real patients.
  • MUST keep all "clinical content" scoped to assessment of a candidate — never surfaced as guidance for a real-world clinical decision.
  • MUST NOT let examiner-confidential material (locked content, concealed construct, model answers, rubric) leak to the candidate in Exam mode.

1. Content governance — clinical-content locking#

  • MUST source every medical fact the patient/AI states from the station's LOCKED sections (S3 patient profile, S5 clinical deep-dive). The AI MAY rephrase for tone; it MUST NOT add, invent, or alter any fact, dose, or safety statement.
  • MUST enforce a hallucination guard: if a candidate asks something not covered by locked content, the SP deflects or stays within locked facts rather than confabulating. (Verified by test T-X-01.)
  • MUST carry an author and a clinical reviewer of record, a version number, and an explicit "ready for implementation" status gate before publication.
  • MUST re-enter clinical review whenever any LOCKED section changes.

2. Critical safety requirements#

Every station MUST enumerate its critical-fail items. Each critical-fail item: - MUST be individually scoreable, and - MUST hard-fail the station when scored 0 — regardless of the aggregate percentage — with the failure surfaced explicitly on the result (never averaged away). (T-SCORE-02, T-BRDG-02.)

Station-level safety rules that MUST be enforced where the station defines them: - Crucial "lie checks" — claims the candidate must make that protect the patient from a dangerous misconception (e.g. ITB: oral baclofen cannot rescue intrathecal withdrawal). Failing to make the check, or affirming the misconception, is a critical fail. - No false reassurance / no blanket approvals (e.g. ITB: never state all pumps are unconditionally MRI-safe). - Contraindication screening before consent where defined. - Safety-netting — return-precautions for red-flag symptoms where defined (e.g. AS: new neurological symptoms or new spinal trauma). - Teach-back is a critical safety item: closing without it is a silent critical fail. (T-CLOSE-01.)

3. Disclosure discipline#

  • MUST keep withheld information clinically accurate (drawn from S3/S4) — controlled disclosure governs timing and granularity, never the truth of a fact.
  • MUST disclose at the granularity asked: a broad question yields one relevant detail; a targeted question yields only that attribute; salient facts stay withheld until elicited. (T-DISC-01/02.)
  • MUST NOT fabricate a clinical detail to satisfy a candidate's question (this is the content lock applied to disclosure).

4. Communication & health-literacy compliance#

  • MUST run the jargon layer for any station whose patient has a non-medical background: flagged-term watch-list, in-character confusion, escalating counter, and communication-domain capping at the 4+ threshold. (T-JARG-.)*
  • MUST treat plain-language communication as scoreable; a candidate who loses the patient to jargon fails the communication domain even if the clinical content is correct.

5. Assessment validity & fairness#

  • MUST vary only the presentation layer across tiers (task concealment, affect, cue explicitness, question difficulty) and MUST NOT vary clinical facts or rubric items by tier. (T-AID-01.) This is the fairness guarantee.
  • MUST declare the station's own scoring domains, tag every rubric item to exactly one domain, and define item weights (or 0/1/2) and level-adaptive weights.
  • MUST normalise raw marks to a 0–100% wheel and define a justified pass threshold (default 65%; programme-configurable). (T-SCORE-03.)
  • MUST score the construct-recognition item only on concealed tiers, and omit it from the explicit-task (Basic) total. (T-AID-02/03.)

6. Remediation governance#

  • MUST treat the Station Report (results / feedback summary) and the Knowledge Bridge (the remediation engine that generates targeted reinforcement) as distinct surfaces — the results page must not be presented or labelled as the Knowledge Bridge.
  • MUST define bridge triggers for the station: domain <50%, any critical-fail item = 0, or (concealed tiers) construct item = 0.
  • MUST map each trigger to named, authored, clinically reviewed reinforcement (mini-cases, MCQs, clinical pearls, framework reviews), with a defined return path that does not immediately re-test the failed skill in isolation.
  • MUST prioritise critical-safety bridges and MUST NOT allow a candidate to pass through a critical-safety failure without remediation. (T-BRDG-04.)

7. Per-station compliance instances#

7.1 PMR-002 — Intrathecal Baclofen Pump (counseling & informed consent)#

Critical-fail set (each must be satisfied / not violated): - Complete withdrawal warning with ER instruction. - Does not state oral baclofen can safely replace intrathecal dosing if the pump fails (the crucial lie check). - Does not give blanket/unconditional MRI approval. - Performs contraindication screening before proceeding to consent. - Does not dismiss the patient's online death-fear; addresses it non-dismissively. - Does not pressure or rush the patient into a decision. - Performs teach-back before closing. - Jargon counter stays below 4 (communication critical fail at ≥4).

Locked facts that must remain accurate: intrathecal dosing at 1/100th–1/300th of oral; withdrawal signs (severe stiffness, high temperature, confusion, hallucinations, seizures); pump logistics (refill 1–6 months, battery 5–7 years); patient must carry pump-ID card. Withheld: mild latex allergy (released on allergy/history elicitation).

7.2 PLX-PMR-AS-001 — Ankylosing Spondylitis (breaking bad news & joint-protection)#

Critical-fail items (must each score ≥1): - Item 5 — delivers the news clearly, plainly, at appropriate pace. - Item 8 — explains spinal fragility and why contact sport must be avoided. - Item 15 — safety-nets for new neurological symptoms or significant new trauma.

Other safety-critical content: contact sport, spinal manipulation, percussion therapy, and cervical traction are absolutely contraindicated and must be conveyed; realistic safe alternatives must be offered (swimming, aquatic therapy, upright cycling, walking).

Construct recognition: item 3 (recognising unprompted that the encounter is breaking bad news, not a routine history) is scored on Intermediate/Advanced only; on Basic the total is 40, not 42.

Pass: 27/42 ≈ 65%, plus no critical-fail item scored 0.

8. Station sign-off checklist#

A station is publishable only when all are ticked, dated, and initialled by the named clinical reviewer.

  • Product framing respected; examiner-confidential material cannot leak in Exam mode.
  • All clinical facts trace to LOCKED S3/S5; hallucination guard verified (T-X-01).
  • Critical-fail items enumerated; each hard-fails at 0 with explicit surfacing.
  • All "lie checks", no-false-reassurance, contraindication, and safety-net rules enforced.
  • Teach-back enforced as a critical safety item.
  • Withheld-info map accurate and elicitation-gated; no confabulation.
  • Jargon watch-list + analogy bank populated; escalation/capping verified.
  • Domains declared and item-tagged; weights + level weights set; threshold justified.
  • Tier presentation varies; facts/rubric do not (fairness check).
  • Bridge triggers mapped to reviewed drills with return paths; critical-safety prioritised.
  • Author, clinical reviewer, version, and "ready" status recorded.

Verification of each requirement is specified in V3_TEST_PLAN.md (test IDs referenced inline above).
